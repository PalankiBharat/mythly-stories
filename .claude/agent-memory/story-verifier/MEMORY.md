# Story Verifier - Persistent Memory

## Key Verification Patterns

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Deity-to-Guest Mapping
- Stories frequently assign specific deity identities to specific disguised forms (e.g., "Brahma appeared as the Brahmana")
- The Bhagavata Purana often does NOT specify which deity took which form -- it says "demigods including X and Y presented themselves as brahmana, shudra, chandala and so on"
- Always check the ACTUAL verse for named deities vs. popular retelling traditions
- SB 9.21.15 names Brahma and Shiva but NOT Vishnu as appearing deities; Vishnu is the Maya source

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Guest Merging
- Stories often merge multiple scripture visitors into fewer characters for dramatic simplicity
- Always count the actual number of visitors in the source text vs. the retelling
- Rantideva story: 4 guests (Brahmana, Shudra, man with dogs, Chandala) -- often merged to 3

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Scripture Chapter Mapping Errors
- Devi Mahatmya = Markandeya Purana chapters 81-93 (13 chapters)
- DM Chapter N = MP Chapter (80 + N). So DM Ch 1 = MP Ch 81, DM Ch 11 = MP Ch 91, DM Ch 13 = MP Ch 93
- Stories frequently claim DM Ch 11 = MP Ch 93 (WRONG -- that's DM Ch 13)
- Always verify chapter correspondence with arithmetic: MP chapter = 80 + DM chapter

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Divine Gift Attribution Errors
- Stories frequently misattribute who gives divine weapons/items to characters
- Kalki Purana: Sword, horse Devadatta, and parrot Shuka are gifts from LORD SHIVA -- NOT from Parashurama
- Parashurama's role in Kalki Purana is specifically as military TRAINER (64 arts, Vedic knowledge)
- SB 12.2.19-20 mentions sword and horse but does NOT specify the giver -- only KP does
- Always verify: Who GIVES the weapon vs. who TRAINS the hero -- these are different roles
- Mother Sumati is named only in Kalki Purana, not in SB 12.2 or VP 4.24

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Cross-Text Detail Attribution
- Panchagni tapas (five fires) is from Kalidasa's Kumara Sambhava Canto 5, NOT from Shiva Purana Parvati Khanda
- Shiva Purana Ch 22 describes seasonal endurance + standing on one leg, but NOT the five-fires framework
- Stories frequently attribute Kumara Sambhava details to Shiva Purana because both cover Parvati's tapas
- Always check: which SOURCE contains a specific detail vs. which source covers the same STORY

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Valmiki vs Tulsidas/Puranic Version Conflation
- Ahalya story has THREE major divergences between Valmiki Ramayana and later traditions:
  - Curse: Invisibility + penance (Valmiki 1-48-30) vs. turned to stone (Padma Purana / Tulsidas)
  - Indra's punishment: Testicle loss (Valmiki 1-48-28) vs. 1000 vulvae->eyes (Brahma/Padma/Skanda Puranas)
  - Liberation: Rama's arrival/entry into hermitage (Valmiki 1-49) vs. foot touching stone (Tulsidas/Padma Purana)
- Stories MUST specify which version they follow; mixing versions is a common error
- In Valmiki, Rama and Lakshmana touch AHALYA's feet after liberation -- not the reverse
- Ahalya's knowledge of Indra: verse 1-48-19 uses "vijnaaya" (knowing), NOT "viditvaa api"
- valmikiramayan.net is an excellent source for verse-level Valmiki Ramayana verification (Sanskrit + translation)

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Pre-Story Framing from Popular Tradition
- Harishchandra story: The "Vasishtha-Vishvamitra debate in Indra's court" is from POPULAR TRADITION, NOT from the Markandeya Purana
- Markandeya Purana Canto VII opens directly with Harishchandra hunting in the forest, encountering Vishvamitra
- The Vasishtha-Vishvamitra quarrel is in Canto IX (AFTER the story), where they fight in bird form
- Wife's name: "Shaivya" in Markandeya Purana; "Taramati" is from popular/later tradition (Bharatendu play etc.)
- The triggering event in MP is Raudra Vighnaraja (demon) creating illusion of women crying, disturbing Vishvamitra's tapas
- Always check: does the scripture TEXT contain a framing element, or is it from popular retelling tradition?

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Source Conflation
- Stories merge brief Devi Mahatmya prophecies with expanded Devi Bhagavata Purana narratives
- DM Ch 11 contains brief future-tense prophecies of incarnations (Shakambhari, Bhramari, etc.)
- Devi Bhagavata Purana Skandha 7 Ch 28 has the EXPANDED narrative with full battle details
- The "tears becoming rivers for nine nights" detail is from DBP, not DM
- Always check which source a specific detail comes from

### Common Hallucination Pattern: Skanda Purana vs Folk Tradition Conflation (Barbarika)
- Barbarika story has TWO distinct layers: Skanda Purana text vs Rajasthani folk tradition
- SKANDA PURANA (Maheshvara-khanda, Kaumarika-khanda, Ch. 59-66): son of Ghatotkacha and Kamakatanikara, ONE special arrow with ash from Goddess Durga, Krishna beheads with Sudarshana Chakra, head made immortal by Goddess Chandika, watches war from hilltop
- FOLK TRADITION (NOT in Skanda Purana): Teen Baan (three arrows from Shiva), vow to fight for losing side, Krishna asking for head as daan/donation, peepal leaf test, names "Maurvi"/"Ahilawati" for mother
- Barbarika is NOT in the Mahabharata at all (not BORI CE, not any recension)
- Khatu Shyam temple (Sikar, Rajasthan) is REAL -- govt registered, founded c. 1027 CE
- dvaipayana.net has reliable scholarly summary of the actual Skanda Purana text
- Stories commonly present the folk version AS IF it is the Skanda Purana version -- always distinguish
