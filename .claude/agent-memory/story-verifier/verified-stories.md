## Story: Jalandhara -- The Demon Born from Shiva's Third Eye
- **Source:** Shiva Purana, Rudra Samhita, Yuddha Khanda, Chapters 13-24; Padma Purana, Uttara Khanda, Chapters 3-18
- **Verdict:** APPROVED
- **Date:** 2026-03-07
- **Issues Found:** None. All 8 claims verified across 8+ independent sources.
- **Key Facts Verified:**
  1. Birth from Shiva's third-eye fire + ocean (Indra's arrogance at Kailash triggered it)
  2. Samudra (ocean god) raised Jalandhara, arranged marriage to Vrinda (daughter of Kalanemi)
  3. Vrinda's chastity = source of invincibility (Brahma's boon / pativrata dharma)
  4. Conquered three worlds, defeated Indra and the devas
  5. Demanded Parvati from Shiva (instigated by Narada)
  6. Vishnu disguised as Jalandhara to break Vrinda's chastity
  7. Vrinda cursed Vishnu to become stone (Shaligrama) -- Padma Purana tradition

- **Key Learning:** Vrinda's curse has TWO versions in different traditions: (a) "become stone" (Shaligrama), (b) "separation from consort" (Rama-Sita). Both are attested.
- **Confidence:** HIGH (8+ independent sources, both scripture references confirmed)

## Story: Kalki Avatar Prophecy
- **Source:** Kalki Purana (primary); Bhagavata Purana Skandha 12.2; Vishnu Purana 4.24
- **Verdict:** CONDITIONALLY APPROVED (Round 1)
- **Date:** 2026-03-07
- **Issues Found:**
  1. Sword attributed to Parashurama -- WRONG. In Kalki Purana, Lord Shiva gives the sword (along with horse Devadatta and parrot Shuka) after being pleased by Kalki's devotion. Parashurama is only the military trainer.
- **What Passed:** Core prophecy, all three scripture references verified at verse level, Shambhala village, Vishnuyasha father, Sumati mother (KP-specific), horse Devadatta (SB 12.2.19), Parashurama as trainer, Kali Yuga degeneration details, all characters
- **Key Verses:**
  - SB 12.2.18: "shambhala-grama-mukhyasya brahmanasya mahatmanah / bhavane vishnuyashasah kalkih pradurbhavishyati"
  - SB 12.2.19-20: Horse Devadatta, sword, killing thieves dressed as kings
  - VP 4.24: "born in the family of Vishnuyashas, an eminent Brahman of Shambala village"

- **Key Learning:** Sumati (mother) is named only in Kalki Purana, not in SB 12.2 or VP 4.24.
- **Key Learning:** Kalki Purana is an Upapurana (not Mahapurana), dated 1500-1700 CE, composed in Bengal.
- **Confidence:** HIGH (8+ independent sources, verse-level verification from vedabase.io and sacred-texts.com)

## Story: Ekalavya and Drona's Gurudakshina
- **Source:** Mahabharata, Adi Parva, Sambhava Parva, Section CXXXIV (Ganguli) / Adhyaya ~123 (BORI)
- **Verdict:** CONDITIONALLY APPROVED
- **Date:** 2026-03-07
- **Issues Found:**
  1. Called "Nishada boy" -- text says "Nishada prince, son of Hiranyadhanus, king of the Nishadas"
  2. "Without injuring" the dog is a popular retelling embellishment -- Ganguli says "pierced with seven arrows"
  3. Source citation "Chapters ~131-134" is overly broad -- story is entirely in Section CXXXIV (134) only
- **What Passed:** Core story fully verified, all characters, clay image, seven arrows, Arjuna's complaint, right thumb demand, "cheerful face and unafflicted heart" response
- **Key Quotes from Ganguli Section CXXXIV:**
  - "a prince named Ekalavya, who was the son of Hiranyadhanus, king of the Nishadas"
  - "Drona...accepted not the prince...seeing that he was a Nishada who might (in time) excel all his high-born pupils"
  - "he made a clay-image of Drona, and began to worship it respectfully"
  - "sent seven arrows into its mouth"
  - "I should like then to have the thumb of thy right hand"
  - "with a cheerful face and an unafflicted heart cut off without ado his thumb"
- **Key Learning:** The dog was NOT uninjured -- the text says "pierced with seven arrows." The "without injuring" claim is from popular retellings, not the Ganguli original.
- **Key Learning:** Ekalavya was a PRINCE (son of a king), not a commoner. The "low-caste" framing is modern interpretation; the text says "Nishada" vs. "high-born pupils."
- **Confidence:** HIGH (6+ independent sources, direct Ganguli text verification via ibiblio.org)

## Story: Ahalya Episode (Claim-Based Verification)
- **Source:** Valmiki Ramayana, Bala Kanda, Sargas 48-49
- **Verdict:** APPROVED WITH NOTES (all core claims pass; advisory corrections only)
- **Date:** 2026-03-07
- **Issues Found:** Two advisory (non-blocking) corrections:
  1. Sanskrit phrase "devaragam viditvaa api" is INCORRECT -- actual verse 1-48-19 uses "vijnaaya" not "viditvaa api"
  2. Liberation mechanism is Rama's ARRIVAL/ENTRY into hermitage, not "feet touching ground" (foot-touch is Tulsidas/Padma Purana version)
- **All 9 Claims Verified:**
  1. Episode in Bala Kanda Sargas 48-49 -- YES
  2. Ahalya = wife of Gautama -- YES
  3. Indra disguised as Gautama -- YES (1-48-18)
  4. Ahalya recognized Indra, consented -- YES (1-48-19, word is "vijnaaya" + "durmedhaa" + "kutuuhalaat")
  5. Gautama cursed both -- YES (1-48-24 through 1-48-30)
  6. Curse = invisibility not stone -- YES (1-48-30: "adrishya sarva bhutaanaam"; stone is Padma Purana/Tulsidas)
  7. Indra lost testicles not 1000 eyes -- YES (1-48-28: "petatuh vrishanau bhumau"; 1000 eyes is Puranic)
  8. Ram testicles grafted -- YES (1-49-6: "meshasya vrishanau grihya shakraayaashu prayacchata")
  9. Goldman translation reliable -- YES (Princeton University Press, 2017 World Sanskrit Award)
- **Key Verses:**
  - 1-48-18: Indra in Gautama's guise
  - 1-48-19: "muni vesham sahasraaksham vijnaaya... durmedhaa deva raaja kutuuhalaat"
  - 1-48-28: Indra's testicles fall
  - 1-48-29-30: Ahalya cursed invisible ("adrishya"), penance in ashes ("bhasma shaayinii")
  - 1-49-6: Ram testicles given to Indra
  - 1-49-16-17: Ahalya manifests when Rama enters hermitage
- **Key Learning:** Valmiki vs Tulsidas/Puranic versions differ on THREE points: (a) invisibility vs stone, (b) testicle loss vs 1000 vulvae/eyes, (c) arrival-based liberation vs foot-touch on stone. Stories MUST specify which version they follow.
- **Key Learning:** The "pada sparsha" (foot-touch) liberation is Tulsidas's Ramcharitmanas (adapted from Padma Purana). Valmiki's version is arrival/entry-based.
- **Key Learning:** In Valmiki, after Ahalya manifests, Rama and Lakshmana touch HER feet -- not the reverse.
- **Confidence:** HIGH (10 searches, 8+ independent sources, verse-level verification)
