An intimate emotional scene inside the ancient hermitage, a beautiful Indian woman (Ahalya) is becoming visible, she is emerging from invisibility like a figure materializing from dust and light, her lower half still fades into the ashen ground while her upper body and face are becoming clear and solid, she is thin from centuries of penance and her simple white sari is grey with ash, her expression shows overwhelming emotion a mixture of relief and quiet tears, her hands are slowly rising from her sides as if waking from an impossibly long sleep, standing a few steps away in the doorway is the silhouette of a young prince (Rama) with a bow on his shoulder backlit by golden morning sunlight that pours into the dark dusty interior, the light from the doorway falls across Ahalya like a benediction, dust motes float in the golden light beams, the ashram interior is bare earth floor with a few clay pots and dried flowers, Indian mythological painting style with the luminosity of Rembrandt chiaroscuro combined with Ajanta cave painting warmth, mood of liberation after unbearable patience and the first moment of grace after centuries of silence. Aspect ratio 1:1, square format.
